About |

Haijing Liu is a PhD candidate in Community and Regional Planning at the School of Architecture, University of Texas at Austin. Her research questions how local climate policies and programs shape the uneven distribution of green job development, as well as access and job quality disparities among different racial and ethnic groups in major metropolitan areas across the USA, using both quantitative and qualitative research methods.

She works on topics in innovation geography, race and ethnicity, and environmental policies in the United States and China. Her background as an architect, combined with my experience as a community advocate, informs my approach to urban planning and policy analysis.

Liu recieved a Master of City Planning from MIT's Department of Urban Studies and Planning, a Master of Architecture from University of Michigan, and a Bachelor of Engineering from Nanjing University in China.
