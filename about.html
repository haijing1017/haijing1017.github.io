ABOUT

My research interests lie at the intersection of innovation geography, climate change, and equity, which I have explored through several peer-reviewed publications and presentations at major conferences such as ACSP and Urban Affairs. I am also deeply involved in community engagement projects, like developing life sciences capabilities in gateway cities and creating a data portal for urban heat advocacy in New York City.

In addition to my research work, I have experience lecturing on research methods, data visualization, survey methods, and regression analysis to graduate students, and I have enjoyed mentoring students on their class projects. My goal is to contribute to the sustainable and equitable development of urban areas by bridging the gap between data-driven insights and practical applications.
